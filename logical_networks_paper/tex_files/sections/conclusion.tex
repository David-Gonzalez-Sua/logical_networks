\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we presented the C-MCPN Framework and Editor, demonstrating that Answer Set Programming can be used to represent and conduct inference over artificial neural network structures, specifically McCulloch-Pitts Networks. Our contribution is twofold: a novel two-layered representation for encoding ANN structure and inference in ASP, and a working, accessible tool that makes this representation practical to construct, visualize, and experiment with. Together, these establish a foundation for future work exploring whether ASP-based learning models can offer meaningful efficiency gains over traditional ANN implementations, an open question we are actively pursuing.
